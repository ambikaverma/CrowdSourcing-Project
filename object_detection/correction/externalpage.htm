INSTRUCTIONS

A smart computer is trying to predict the location of a certain object and needs your help! Your job is to "correct" the computer's bounding box by drawing over it with your own.

  • You will be shown 10 images one by one. Each image will have a predicted label (red text) and bounding box (orange rectangle) from the computer. Verify if the predicted label is correct by choosing Yes or No. Draw the correct bounding box by clicking and dragging over the image. If the predicted bounding box is correct, click the copy button to copy the predicted box.
  • Draw your bounding box such that the box completely covers the object (including obscured parts) but does not cut off any part of it. If the orange box covers multiple instances, draw a box around the most prominent object.
  • Press the < Prev and Next > buttons to navigate between images. If the image does not load, click the red button. Once you have reached the last image, press Next > to optionally provide feedback and submit your work.

Is the orange box covering a(n) ? If not, what is it covering?

Click and drag over the image to draw an improved bounding box or click the 'copy' button if the prediction is already well-defined. Click the 'reset' button below to start over. Be careful not to drag your cursor outside the image.
